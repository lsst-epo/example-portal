Cataloging the Solar System

Investigate Objects from the Birth of Our Solar System

Asteroids and KBOs are of particular interest because they have changed little since the solar system formed 4.5 billion years ago. They are some of the oldest objects in the solar system, and their study can reveal information on how the solar system formed.

Fastest Discovery of Asteroids & Kuiper Belt Objects

The LSST alert stream will provide millions of notifications on variable phenomena on timescales not studied before by other telescopes.

Identify Valuable Asteroids to Visit with Space Craft

Some asteroids will be of interest because they are targets to visit with spacecraft, or even future mining opportunities.

Discover Comets at the Distant Edge of Our Solar System

LSST will be especially good at finding KBOs and comets, objects which are faint and move slowly because they are on the outer edges of the solar system.

Tell us about your science interests

My background is in small bodies of the solar system, especially comet activity, volatile composition, and main-belt comets. I am also interested in the detection and characterization of exoplanets.

When did you know you wanted to be an astronomer?

In high school, I had a teacher who was very excited about astronomy. She encouraged us to make an afterschool club and we would meet in the evenings to view the night sky through her telescope. My love of astronomy started on pretty cold with a group of my friends. This is still something I love about astronomy.
