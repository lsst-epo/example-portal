NGSS Assessment Techniques (from NSTA)

Students can demonstrate competency with tasks such as:

  • developing and refining models;
  • generating, discussing and analyzing data;
  • constructing spoken and written scientific explanations;
  • engaging in evidence-based argumentation; and
  • reflecting on their own understanding.

Options for Summary and Assessment

  1. Use the LSST Assessment video and discuss it with your class.
    • During the video pauses, an effective assessment strategy is to make use of some form of free response system to query the class about which students had the right idea. Options for this may be the use of Kahoot, Clickers, Socrative, Poll Everywhere, or student response cards such as seen on TILT or The Teacher Toolkit
    • Alternatively, you can use an Academically Productive Discussion.
  2. Ask students to complete a narrative summary in their Jupyter notebooks and submit it to you.
  3. Have students complete a Driving Question Board or KLEWS chart.
  4. Use white boards or posters to have students present their findings. This may be used in conjunction with a Gallery Walk.
  5. Use the set of question prompts that are aligned with the custom NGSS assessment rubric for the activity.
  6. Create your own rubric from this generic NGSS assessment template.
