Technology Enabling LSST Discoveries

10 Year Survey with Petabyes of Images & Data Products

A team of astronomers has taken a close look at a nearby galaxy — and discovered an unusual structure that sheds light on supermassive black holes' relationships with their host galaxies.

Alert Stream Notifications of Real-Time Changes

The alert stream will provide millions of notifications on variable phenomena on timescales not previously studied before by other telescopes. Scientists around the world will be able to follow up with additional observations of time domain events with higher magnification telescopes.

Big Data Analysis Tools Lorem Ipsum Dolor

A survey of the scale of LSST requires a modern, high-performance, scalable, data processing and analysis system. The LSST software stack is open source, and comprised of modular codes ranging from science pipelines to web user interfaces.

Largest Digital Camera Ever Constructed

The LSST camera is the largest digital camera ever constructed. At about 5.5 ft by 9.8 ft it's roughly the size of a small car and weighs almost 6200 lbs (2800 kg). It is a large-aperture, wide-field optical imager capable of viewing light from the near ultraviolet to near infrared (0.3-1 urn) wavelengths.

Meet Astronomers, Data Scientists & Engineers

MECHANICAL ENGINEER

Kairn Brannon

The LSST Calibration Hardware System Design & Development @kairn

Tell us about your work

Most recently I have been working on a collimated beam projector for precise telescope calibration. We are building this to ensure that the images that LSST takes are not impacted by stray light effects and reflections and ghosting.

When did you know you wanted to work on telescopes?

I have a background in mechanical engineering and I have created all sorts of robotics projects — I recently built a portable drone charing station that needs little to no operator maintenance. My college advisor, Christopher Stubbs, is a professor of both physics and astronomy and through him I have become interested in robotic telescopes.
