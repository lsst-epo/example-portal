Experience the Changing Cosmos

Capture Massive Changes in the Universe Every Night

LSST will be taking pictures of the entire available sky every 3 days for 10 years, enabling astronomers to study the changing brightness of millions of objects on short timescales.

See Stars Evolve, Interact & Change in Real-Time

The LSST alert stream will provide millions of notifications on variable phenomena on timescales not studied before by other telescopes.

Observe Changes in Supermassive Black Holes

AGN variability. "AGN" are the active centers of galaxies. AGN are powered by supermassive black holes that are accreting star, dust, and gas near them.

Discover New Classes of Stars

Because LSST will be sensitive to a new time domain, it is expected it will discover new classes of variable stars and new kinds of events for stars that rapidly change their brightness.

Meet Astronomers, Data Scientists & Engineers

ASTRONOMER

Lucianne Walkowicz

Transients and Variable Stars Collaboration Co-Chair @shaka_lulu

Tell us about your science interests

I think that LSST is a going to be a fundamental change in how astronomy is done – the time domain aspect and immediate data availability go beyond even the large surveys. It will be a very exciting time for those of us involved in the field for a while to learn to think about our research processes differently, while new students coming into the field will take this “new” landscape at face value.

When did you know you wanted
to be an astronomer?

I have always been curious about life on other planets. When I realized that the study of astronomy could help us find other habitable planets my curiosity and career became the same thing.
