Purpose

  • The Forum will provide a place where users of LSST Jupyter notebooks and instructional videos can exchange ideas and support. It is specific in scope and not intended as a place to discuss general astronomy knowledge or teaching techniques.
  • One important function is to provide a way for users to ask for specific help beyond the scope of the knowledge base or tutorial videos. This may be technical or pedagogical in nature. This has a two-way benefit, because it could inform LSST staff of how to reinforce the knowledge base, notebook instructions, or tutorials.
  • In the early days (during Construction) of the forum, it may provide valuable feedback as teachers and independent users begin to try out prototypes. It will help to inform staff development needs.
  • Users may wish to collaborate on joint reach projects. This may extend to scientists working with interested students, amateurs or teachers on a long-term project, such as locating and characterizing an unusual or new type of object. In some cases, this approach may be more suitable than, or may help to inform, the creation of a citizen science project.
  • Users may wish to share a modified notebook or assessment tool they have created.
  • In addition, there is a case to be made for synergy. Discussions can fire up the enthusiasm level and also serve to stimulate new ideas, as well as forge relationships between users. These in turn may serve as mentoring or collaboration opportunities. In some cases, local area cadres of users may even host presentations or training to share LSST EPO products with others.
  • Users will be primarily educators and LSST staff members, but there are additional people who are likely to be interested in joining:
    • Data users: these are not the experienced API users who have mastery of Python and will write their own notebooks or code to access our data, but rather the "keyboard amateurs" who are interested in using the Jupyter notebooks we have designed, either as part of Formal Education, or perhaps to satisfy a particular interest, such as amateur astronomers who are variable star observers.
    • Another subset would be home-schooled, advanced or independent students either at the high school or college level.
    • Leaders of informal education may also become a significant user group, though that are not the intended primary audience. These may be scout or 4H leaders, STEM group or club moderators, science center or park personnel. The forum may be especially useful for them, since they would not typically attend professional development.

Threads and Posts

One of the advantages of a forum discussion is that posts can be organized by topics and threads. This enables new forum users to quickly search for items of interest, and helps to eliminate redundant posts. Threads can be bumped in order of popularity or newness.

Badging

A badging system could be incorporated into user accounts. Badges serve as a motivational tool, and also help to inform other users as the level of experience of the other users. For instance, badges may reflect the number of posts a user has made, or a certification level (trainer, educator, student, staff, etc.). Perhaps they could display the number of Jupyter notebooks a user has successfully executed. Another useful piece of information might be the date they first registered, "User since 2022". Teachers could earn badges for activities used, forum participation, spreading the word about LSST (certified trainer, activities contributed, popularizer, research teacher (works with students on independent projects), coder, "4 pillars badge" (someone who has completed one investigation in each of the 4 science domains of LSST). Scientists could also earn badges.
