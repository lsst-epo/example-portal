Origin and Fate of the Universe

Explore the Unknown 96% of Our Universe

Dark matter and dark energy make up about 96% mass and energy of the Universe but we cannot directly detect or see either.

The Past, Present & Future of the Universe

LSST will provide data necessary to test important ideas and theories about dark matter, dark energy, and their roles in the past, present, and future of the Universe.

Connect Theoretical & Observational Astronomy

Dark matter neither emits nor absorbs light but is required to explain the rotation rates of galaxies as well as account for the amount of gravity needed to hold galaxy clusters together.

Investigate the Ongoing Expansion of the Universe

Dark energy is what is thought to be causing the accelerating expansion of the Universe

Meet Astronomers, Data Scientists & Engineers

ASTRONOMER

Rachel Bean

Co-Chair of the LSST Dark Energy Science Collaboration @CornellAstro

Tell us about your work

Dark energy could be a strange new type of matter or it could be evidence that we don’t fully understand how gravity behaves on cosmic scales. I am interested in how can we use different types of astrophysical measurements to distinguish  between competing theories.

When did you know you wanted to be an astronomer?

The first time I saw the rings of Saturn through a telescope, I was hooked. I didn’t know yet that I wanted to be an astronomer, but I knew that there was something incredible about looking so deeply into darkness and finding entirely new places.
